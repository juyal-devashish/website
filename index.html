About Me

I’m a graduate student who’s slowly growing into an engineer; the kind who cares about what’s happening under the hood, not just what the model outputs.

That mindset took shape during my undergrad at MNIT Jaipur and deepened during my graduate studies at the University of Michigan -- Ann Arbor. Along the way, curiosity turned into a habit of building systems that make sense of real-world data — especially using machine learning. Recently, my work has centered on understanding when and why models make decisions, with a blend of language models. I’ve worked on ML-based sequential detection, watermarking of LLMs, distillation of small language models, and even a causality-driven trading engine. Across projects, the goal stays the same: making models more reliable, interpretable, and grounded.
